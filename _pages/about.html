--- layout: page title: About permalink: /about/ priority: 0.9 intro: | Hi! I'm Paulo Vitor Itaboraí. I consider myself to be a music technologist and multimedia artist. I like to study. I'm currently pursuing to be in a bridge between contemporary art, physics and computation. Some of the fields that I'm interested in are: Audio (and video) signal processing in real-time, new musical interfaces, computer-assisted composition, mixed music, sound spatialization, improvisation and experimentation, instrument construction, computer music, quantum computing. I also believe that these technologies and art expressions should be developed and used in a more 'organic' approach, avoiding unrestrained growth and acceleration of our society. My main research field today is Quantum Computer Music. Currently, I'm a remote visiting research fellow at the [Interdisciplinary Centre for Computer Music Research](http://cmr.soc.plymouth.ac.uk/), participating in the [QuTune Project](https://iccmr-quantum.github.io) - developing tools and didactic material for audio signal processing on quantum computers. I have a bachelor's degree in Physics at the [University of São Paulo](http://portal.if.usp.br/ifusp/en). I participated in the [Computer Music Group - IME/USP](https://compmus.ime.usp.br/en/home), during my undergrad studies. I was also a "Non-Degree" student of Electroacoustic Composition at [Studio PANaroma](http://www.flomenezes.mus.br/panaroma/index_panaroma.html), from where I developed many of my [technical-artistic collaborations](/projects_collaborations).
